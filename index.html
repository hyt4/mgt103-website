About the project

We're a team of three students — Nick, Ariel, and Mustafa, working to improve the dining experience at Peirce Dining Hall. Our research found that long lines, unclear station layouts, and unpredictable crowds push students to skip meals or eat off campus entirely. Our solutions focus on three key changes: introducing a conveyor-belt grill to speed up burger output, moving chicken tenders to a self-serve island to reduce queue overlap, and exploring pre-ordering to cut checkout time. Each idea is grounded in real student feedback and tested assumptions. Our goal is simple, help students eat faster, stress less, and actually enjoy their meal break.
